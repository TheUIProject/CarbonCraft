Cylinder Deactivation (CDA)

CDA involves shutting off one or more engine cylinders during light-load conditions, such as cruising or idling. This reduces pumping losses, friction, and heat generation, leading to: Improved fuel efficiency (up to 20%) Lower emissions (CO2, NOx, and HC) Reduced engine wear



Variable Displacement (VD):

VD engines can change their displacement by deactivating or reactivating cylinders or using variable valve timing. This allows the engine to: Operate at optimal efficiency for varying loads Reduce emissions and fuel consumption Improve performance and responsiveness



Emission Control

Catalytic Converters: Devices that use catalysts to convert harmful pollutants into less harmful substances.
Particulate Filters: Devices that capture and filter out particulate matter (PM) from diesel engine exhaust.
Selective Catalytic Reduction (SCR) Systems: Systems that inject a reducing agent into the exhaust stream to reduce NOx emissions.
Diesel Oxidation Catalysts (DOCs): Devices that oxidize CO and HC emissions from diesel engines.
Exhaust Gas Recirculation (EGR) Systems: Systems that recirculate a portion of the exhaust gas back into the engine cylinders to reduce NOx emissions.

Engine Downsizing

Turbocharging: Uses a turbine to compress air, forcing more air into the engine, and increasing power output.
Supercharging: Uses a mechanically driven compressor to force more air into the engine, increasing power output.
Direct Injection: Injects fuel directly into the engine's cylinders, improving combustion efficiency and power output.
Variable Valve Timing: Optimizes engine performance by adjusting valve timing and lift.
